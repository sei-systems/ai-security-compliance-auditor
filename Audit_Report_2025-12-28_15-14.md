## Audit Readiness Report: Daily Compliance Check - 2025-12-27 to 2025-12-28

**Prepared For:** Security & Compliance Committee
**Date:** 2025-12-28
**Auditor:** Senior SOC2 & NIST 800-53 Auditor

---

### 1. Executive Summary

This report presents an analysis of security logs from 2025-12-27 to 2025-12-28, identifying areas of non-compliance and potential security vulnerabilities in relation to SOC2 Common Criteria and NIST 800-53 controls. The analysis reveals several critical security incidents, including widespread unauthorized privileged access attempts, persistent brute-force login attempts, and recurring operational stability issues affecting a core database cluster. Immediate action is required to investigate and remediate the high-risk findings.

### 2. Summary of Findings

The review of logs highlights significant issues across several control domains:

*   **High Risk:** A high volume of "Unauthorized sudo attempt on /etc/shadow" events involving multiple user accounts (admin, webapp, db_user, root, jdoe) indicates either a severe compromise, a widespread misconfiguration, or malicious insider activity. This is the most critical finding and requires immediate incident response.
*   **Medium Risk:** Persistent "Failed login attempt" events from multiple external IP addresses targeting various user accounts (admin, root, jdoe, webapp, db_user) point to active brute-force or credential stuffing attacks. An internal IP also showed failed login attempts for 'admin'.
*   **Medium Risk:** Numerous "Connection timeout from database_cluster_01" errors suggest underlying stability, performance, or network issues with the database, potentially impacting service availability.

### 7. General Recommendations & Audit Readiness

The current log sample indicates significant areas of concern that require immediate attention to achieve a satisfactory level of SOC2 and NIST 800-53 compliance.

1.  **Strengthen Incident Response Plan (SOC2 CC7.2, NIST AU-6):** The sheer volume of critical and error logs suggests that current incident detection, analysis, and response mechanisms may be insufficient or not properly implemented. An immediate review and activation of the incident response plan is paramount.
2.  **Implement Privileged Access Management (PAM) (SOC2 CC6.1, NIST AC-2):** Given the repeated unauthorized sudo attempts on `/etc/shadow`, a PAM solution should be implemented to centralize, control, and monitor all privileged access. This would ensure granular control, session recording, and multi-factor authentication for critical operations.
3.  **Enhance Access Control Mechanisms (SOC2 CC6.1, NIST AC-2):**
    *   **MFA Everywhere:** Mandate Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for all user accounts, especially those with privileged access and remote access points.
    *   **Account Lockout:** Implement strong account lockout policies for failed login attempts to mitigate brute-force attacks.
    *   **Principle of Least Privilege:** Conduct a thorough review of all user accounts and service accounts to ensure they only have the minimum necessary privileges. Specifically, review `sudoers` configurations for any over-provisioned permissions.
4.  **Improve Log Monitoring & Alerting (SOC2 CC7.2, NIST AU-6):** The logs indicate multiple suspicious activities that should trigger immediate high-priority alerts within a Security Information and Event Management (SIEM) system. Review and fine-tune SIEM rules for:
    *   Unauthorized `sudo` attempts on sensitive files (e.g., `/etc/shadow`, `/etc/passwd`).
    *   Multiple failed login attempts from a single source IP over a short period.
    *   Repeated failed login attempts on privileged accounts (e.g., `root`, `admin`).
    *   Persistent service availability issues (e.g., database connection timeouts).
5.  **Perform Regular Vulnerability Assessments & Penetration Testing (SOC2 CC6.1):** Proactive testing can help identify misconfigurations or vulnerabilities that attackers might exploit, such as those leading to unauthorized sudo attempts.
6.  **Conduct Comprehensive Root Cause Analysis (NIST AU-6):** For both the security incidents and operational errors, a thorough root cause analysis must be performed to understand *why* these events are occurring and to implement permanent preventive measures rather than just reactive fixes.
7.  **Review Network Segmentation & Firewall Rules (SOC2 CC6.1):** Investigate the source IPs of failed login attempts. Implement geo-blocking or IP reputation-based blocking if these IPs are from known malicious regions or threat intelligence feeds. Ensure internal networks are properly segmented to contain potential compromises.

Addressing these findings with urgency is critical for maintaining a strong security posture and demonstrating readiness for SOC2 and NIST 800-53 compliance audits. Continued monitoring and regular review of logs will be essential.
